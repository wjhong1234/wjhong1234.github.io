Define

Icarus is a mobile game on the Android OS platform, created for Tina Huynh as her personal button of delight. The game follows Icarus the Cookie as he finds his friends while avoiding dangerous spiders. Navigate Icarus along the rainbow in the clouds, finding 30 of his cookie friends. But be careful-- not everything on the rainbow is pleasant!


Created in Android Studio, this game was designed for Tina Huynh, tailored towards the things she enjoys. With a focus on her love of video games, pastel colours, and cookies, Icarus is meant to combine all of the things that delight her into a single button.


Ideation

The button’s usage was geared toward making Tina feel joy. As such, initial brainstorming of her button led to concepts pertaining to what is perceived as ‘cute’ (ie. bright pastel colors) or ideas that would remind her of her childhood home of Red Deer.


Variations

Ultimately, her love of video games led me to brainstorm possible fun games, keeping in mind that it had to be implemented in approximately a week.


Of the several ideas for smaller scale games, I decided upon a game similar to the classic ‘Racer’ game.


Planning

A game needs objectives and obstacles, and to keep things consistent, I didn’t want to stray too far from the cookie idea. As such, the objectives became to collect cookies as well, and I gave them all faces to give them a bit of personality. I decided to name the game 'Icarus', since Tina told me she loved mythology and stories, and felt that the name was fitting with the overall feeling of the game. Thinking about what she hates, I decided that bugs would be a good obstacle for the game. Asides from the music and sounds, all assets were created by me.


Initial Themes

I wanted to incorporate things that Tina loved as much as possible. After interviewing her, I found that she enjoyed baking, especially cookies, so I decided that was a good choice for the game.

Final Product

The final product is called "Icarus", an Android Studio project created for my Human-Computer Interactions II class.


Starting Screen

A cookie button that starts the game.

Game Activity

Player drags the cookie around to collect other cookies, while avoiding spiders.

Ending

When the game ends, either in a victory or loss, the game can be restarted by pressing the cookie again.
