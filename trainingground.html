Define

Interactive Horror Training is a Mobile Virtual Reality Application that can help a user get accustomed to common horror movie tropes, such as jump scares and extremely suspenseful situations. The app has the user in the center of an uncomfortable environment, with eerie music and sounds playing around them. Occasionally, suspenseful situations or a jump scare happens, but instead of a monster or ghost, a kitten or other cute creature will appear instead!


Ideation

Some backstory: I personally cannot watch horror movies. If I watch a horror movie, it will haunt me for years to come! It’s been years since I watched a horror movie, so in an attempt to train myself and others to watch horror movies without fear, I decided to create this application.


As seen in the gallery below, my ideation started with several unrelated ideas. I found some similarities between all the ideas however, such as external controllers (usually other mobile devices), gestures or movements, and environment exploration. After narrowing down some ideas, I realized many of them didn’t draw my interest as much as my horror idea, which allowed the user to immersively stand within a horror video.


Idea #1: Bird Landscape Viewer

The Bird Landscape view would allow the user to view landscapes or building sites from a literal bird-eye view.

Idea #2: Immersive Interactions

An interactive world with the goal of encouraging and motivating the user to do their work or practice by creating a world where they are more engaged than in reality.

Idea #3: 3rd Person Horror Experience

With a camera attached to their backs, the users can experience a horror movie in 3rd person, seeing all the shadows and stuff behind them.

Idea #4: Proximity Controls

This idea for video controlling gestures would allow the user to control the video they watch with hand gestures. Another person could also see what they are viewing, as the connected device is strapped to their chest.

Idea #5: Video Dance Controls

The user can use dance moves to controls their basic video functions. Good for exericising, since the user is likely already standing for VR.

Idea #6: Explore VR

Exploring the world could be much easier. Identifying certain items and cool parts of the world, with the information about that world ready to be displayed for the user.

Idea #7: Inception VR

Allows the user to view multiple videos at the same time, in 360. A second device controls the perspective of the inner videos.

Idea #8: Horserider

This app would allow you to explore the world on horseback, no matter where you go.

Idea #9: Monster Simulator

Using a controller, allows the user to act as if they were a specific monster, and live in a world as that monster.

Idea #10: Be A Pumpkin

This application allows the user to 'turn into' a pumpkin anywhere in the world. Users would share their pumpkin's view on an app, and other users can connect to them.

Idea #11: HelpVR

Allows people to immediately contact emergency or medical professionals in real life, getting advice and instructions while help is on the way.

Variations

After talking to some peers, I had another brainstorming session alone, combining, updating, or adding to certain ideas. I ended up with another list of ideas. Some of the feelings I wanted to get from these new ideas were suspenseful, creepy, and eerie. While looking over these ideas, it occurred to me that I would have been too scared to test and develop for any of them except for a couple. I found that the ones that didn’t test my courage too much were the ones where I was the monster, or where the goal was to help me become braver.


I chose the latter idea, and started designing how I wanted it to be like. I took some things from previous ideas to help this one come along, and the final design became Interactive Horror Training.


Variation #1: Flash Light View

A flashlight is used to determine where in the actual video the user can see clearly, while the rest is dark.

Variation #2: Haunted House Rush

User would physically mimic running while their avatar dashes through a haunted house filled with jump scares and monsters.

Variation #3: Innocent Bystander

The user is sitting in a park, watching incredibly strange things happen around them. The more panicked you are, the weirder it gets.

Variation #4: Monster Simulator v2

User is a monster, using other devices as the controls, and the mic as another aspect of controlling how scary the user is.

Variation #5: Multiple Player Horror Maze

One Player is stuck in a maze, another is a guide. The guide can only see so much, but cannot talk to the user vocally. Instead to communicate, the two users must use pings and small map signals. Monsters lurk everywhere.

Variation #6: Scary House VR

Much like the Innocent Bystander idea, but instead, this idea features more traditionally scary things. More ghosts, less weird.

Variation #7: Fake Surgery

One user is a patient and the other is a surgeon. The patient is tied up for extra effect. Would probably cause squeemishness.

Variation #8: Scary Monster Survival

There area bunch of scary monsters and you must keep yourself alive. The more scared you are, the harder it is.

Variation #9: Humming Light

Humming increases the amount of light in the video. Consistent humming is the key... and water. Stay hydrated.

Variation #10: Accidents

Life scarring accidents happen, where you hurt someone by accident, or a situation occurs where you cannot do anything. Would cause extreme distress.

Variation #11: Scary Night

Heartrate effects the environment, augmented reality. This idea differs from the others because of the augmented reality bit. It focuses on manipulating the actual environment rather than creating one.

Variation #12: Scare Training

Scare Training allows the user to get accustomed to jump scares. Horror movies can now be a thing in my life.

Development

Development was done in the Unity Game Engine. I initially thought about using another device as a controller for a flashlight object, that would allow the user to control which parts of the video are visible, but because of time constraints, implementation of that was not possible. Instead, I had a light where the user was looking, while everywhere else was dark.


This experience is much better with headphones on, as the surrounding sounds would be much more immersive compared to a mono speaker on most phones, and better without external noises.


For events that were not jumps scares, I hoped that they would add to the eerie atmosphere, making it feel more like the user was stuck in the middle of a forgotten nowhere, rather than some place safe. The goal was to build suspense before a bigger event occurred.


Final Product

The final product is called "VR Horror Training", a Mobile VR project created for my Human-Computer Interactions II class.
