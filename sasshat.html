Define

Sass hat is a device that reminds you about the things you forget, with an attitude. It speaks to the wearer, either “complimenting” them when they never forget things, or scolding the wearer when they leave without an important item.


The design of the cap is simple, yet charming, and has tons of personality. The voice of Sasshat was meant to entertain the user, while still being effective at helping the user remember the most important items in life.


The SassHat is both an accessory and a tool, helping you to remember your important tasks while also looking like you’re from the future. It acts like a sassy friend following you around, ensuring you do your chores and helping you remember little things.


Ideation

The planning stage for this project was difficult, as I didn't really know what could be done with wearable tech. I had no previous knowledge of its limitations and what not, so I often thought of things that later down the line felt out of place, or didn't follow a theme asides from it being 'wearable'.


The question I came to ask was, what problems did I have in regular everyday life that could be solved by a device? Or what could make this problem more bearable?

Concepts & Refinement

For this project, there was no specific client to cater my design towards. However, I didn’t want to design a product for everyone-- or else it would be interesting to no one. So I chose to design for someone I knew really well.


I began designing for myself. This allowed me to get as much information I could about what I needed in the limited time we had. I’m a pretty lazy person, and I like to sleep, and not reach for anything too far away from me. I’m also incredibly forgetful, and I tend to ignore the reminders I write down. Following these simple guidelines, I began designing accessories that could aid me in my daily life.


Concept #1: Super Duper Versatile Glasses

Created for those who have vision problems, this pair of glasses lets you switch between several modes, including sunglasses, gaming, reading, and even night vision.

Concept #2: Earbud Earrings

For those who already wear earrings and find that sometimes, earbuds can be an obstruction, this pair of earbuds combine jewelery and earbuds so you won't have to carry them around.

Concept #3: Life-Sack Cowl

A piece of gear designed for those who want to stay comfy and stylish, this cowl provides warmth, safety, anything you need to feel comfortable. Can be used as a personal heater or even a pillow.

Concept #4: Reminder Clip

For forgetful people, the Reminder Clip would remind the user by buzzing everytime they forget something at home.

Some of the more interesting ideas included a safety cowl and glasses that changed states depending on the situation it was in. But the idea I decided to develop further was the reminder clip. The initial idea of the reminder clip was a clip that vibrates when you forget something that you normally have with you, such as your phone or wallet.


Over time that idea developed into a hat, and once the kinks were ironed out, it became something more than just a hat that vibrates.


Components & Assets

To build the Sasshat, I had to visit the local electronics shop to grab several things that were not included in the kit provided to us by our professor. It ended up only costing me around 25 dollars.


  • Arduino R3
  • 12 LED NeoPixel Ring
  • An old cap
  • 1 Transistor
  • Lots of wires
  • Thread
  • Electric Tape

I asked my friend, Aerjay Italia, to do voiceovers for me. The programming was all done in C++, with some C#.
